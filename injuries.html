Badminton Injuries & Stretching

Learn about common injuries and simple stretches to stay safe on court.

Common badminton injuries

  • Ankle sprain: Sudden changes of direction and landing from jumps.
  • Knee pain: Repeated lunges and fast stops can irritate joints.
  • Shoulder strain: Overhead smashes and clears done with poor form or without warm-up.
  • Elbow pain: “Tennis elbow” from repeated racket swings and gripping too hard.
  • Lower back pain: Bending, twisting, and reaching for low shots.

Why stretching matters

  • Improves flexibility: Lets joints move smoothly in every direction.
  • Helps prevent injuries: Warmer muscles are less likely to strain or tear.
  • Boosts performance: Quicker, more controlled movements on court.
  • Aids recovery: Reduces stiffness after training or matches.
Stretch gently and slowly. You should feel a stretch, not sharp pain.

Useful stretches for badminton

Calf stretch

Face a wall, step one leg back, heel down, and lean forward until you feel a stretch in the back calf.

Quadriceps stretch

Stand on one leg, hold the other ankle and gently pull the heel toward your buttocks. Keep knees close together.

Hamstring stretch

Sit with one leg straight and the other bent. Reach toward the toes of the straight leg, keeping your back long.

Shoulder stretch

Bring one arm across your chest and hold it with the other arm just above the elbow.

Wrist & forearm stretch

Extend one arm forward, palm up. Use the other hand to gently pull the fingers down and back.

Hold each stretch for about 15–20 seconds, breathe normally, and never bounce.

Simple safety tips

  • Warm up first: 3–5 minutes of light jogging or skipping before stretching.
  • Wear proper shoes: Use court shoes with good grip to protect ankles and knees.
  • Listen to your body: Stop if you feel sharp or sudden pain.
  • Cool down: Stretch again lightly after playing to reduce stiffness.
For learning purposes only. Always ask a coach or PE teacher if you are unsure.
